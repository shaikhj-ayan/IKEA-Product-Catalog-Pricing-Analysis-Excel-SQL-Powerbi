# 📖 Overview

This project analyzes IKEA product data to understand how products are priced across different categories.

The dataset was cleaned using Excel and SQL, then analyzed using SQL queries. A Power BI dashboard was created to show pricing patterns, category performance, and product insights.

---

## ❗ Business Problem

IKEA has a large number of products, but pricing is not easy to understand across categories.

Key problems:

* Different categories follow different pricing styles
* Hard to identify which categories are premium or budget
* Some categories have a very wide price range
* No clear view of how products are distributed by price
* Difficult to make pricing decisions without proper analysis

👉 **Core Problem Statement:**
**How are IKEA products priced across categories, and how can pricing be better structured to improve clarity and decision making?**

---

# 🗂 Dataset

* Source: Hugging Face
* Dataset: IKEA US Products 2025
* Around 30,000 products
* Includes title, price, category, subcategory, description, and more

### Data Preparation

* Cleaned formatting issues in Excel
* Fixed column names and data types in SQL
* Removed duplicates
* Handled missing values
* Standardized category and subcategory names
* Cleaned product titles
* Fixed special characters and encoding issues

Final dataset was clean and ready for analysis.

# 📊 Dashboard & Key Insights

The Power BI dashboard focuses on pricing patterns, category performance, and product distribution.

---

### 📈 Overview

![Overview](https://github.com/shaikhj-ayan/IKEA-Product-Catalog-Pricing-Analysis-Excel-SQL-Powerbi/blob/main/image/overview.png)

**Insights:**

* Total product value is **₹10.46M**, showing a large catalog size
* There are **28 categories**, giving wide product variety
* Average product price is **₹342.89**, which is quite low
* Most expensive product is **₹4,499**, showing a limited premium range

👉 **Finding:**
IKEA mainly focuses on **affordable pricing**, with only a few high-priced products.

---

### 🥧 Category Share (Market Distribution)

![Category Share](https://github.com/shaikhj-ayan/IKEA-Product-Catalog-Pricing-Analysis-Excel-SQL-Powerbi/blob/main/image/product_segment_market_share.png)

**Insights:**

* **Kitchen, appliances & supplies** dominates with ~57% share
* Other categories like sofas, storage, and beds have smaller shares
* Category contribution is not evenly distributed

👉 **Finding:**
Few categories control most of the catalog value.

---

### 📦 Top Categories by Total Value

![Top Categories](https://github.com/shaikhj-ayan/IKEA-Product-Catalog-Pricing-Analysis-Excel-SQL-Powerbi/blob/main/image/top_5_category.png)

**Insights:**

* Kitchen category leads with **₹5.2M total value**
* Sofas and storage categories are second and third
* Beds and tables have much lower contribution

👉 **Finding:**
Revenue is heavily dependent on **top 2–3 categories**.

### 📉 Category Price vs Product Count

### 📊 Category-wise Price Comparison

![Category Pricing](https://github.com/shaikhj-ayan/IKEA-Product-Catalog-Pricing-Analysis-Excel-SQL-Powerbi/blob/main/image/category_wise_average_price.png)

## 🧠 Overall Dashboard Insight

* IKEA mainly focuses on **budget-friendly products**
* Most products are priced below ₹500
* Only a few premium products exist
* Some categories dominate total value
* Pricing is uneven across categories

👉 **Final Insight:**
IKEA follows a **low-price, high-volume strategy**, but needs better pricing balance and clear category positioning.

## 🚀 Final Recommendations

Based on the analysis:

### 1. Improve Pricing Consistency

* Reduce very large price gaps within the same category
* Keep pricing more structured

---

### 2. Clear Category Positioning

* Define which categories are premium and which are budget
* Maintain clear pricing levels

---

### 3. Balance Product Mix

* Keep a balance between budget, mid-range, and premium products
* Avoid too many low-priced or high-priced products in one category

---

### 4. Strengthen Premium Value

* High-priced products should clearly show better quality or features
* Improve product description for premium items

---

### 5. Use Data for Pricing Decisions

* Use pricing data regularly to adjust product strategy
* Monitor category performance over time

---

## 🧠 Final Insight

> IKEA pricing varies a lot across categories, and some categories lack clear structure. Better pricing balance and clear positioning can improve customer understanding and business decisions.
